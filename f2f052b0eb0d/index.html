    The plan

    What Dad/Mom have been doing — same plan for today.

    • Motrin (ibuprofen) every ~6 hours as needed for fever or discomfort.
    • Cool fluids — ice water, popsicles, whatever she'll take.
    • Lukewarm cloth / cool compress on forehead, neck, armpits if she feels hot. Avoid ice baths — they cause shivering, which raises internal temp back up.
    • Light clothing, keep room comfortable (not freezing).
    • Rest. Movies, books, low-energy activities are fine.
    • Wait ~1 hour after a dose for the fever to come down. Expect it to land around 99–101°F. If it doesn't budge, log it — that's useful info for Dad/Mom.

    When to call Dad/Mom (now)

    If any of these show up, don't wait — call right away.

    • Fever over 104°F that doesn't come down with motrin.
    • Hard to wake up, very limp, or not making eye contact.
    • Trouble breathing — fast, noisy, ribs sucking in, lips/fingernails bluish.
    • Seizure (full-body shaking, eyes rolled back, unresponsive).
    • Stiff neck + bad headache, or a rash that doesn't blanch (press a glass against it — if you can still see the spots, that's a 911 sign).
    • No wet diaper / no pee in 8+ hours — signs of dehydration.
    • Won't drink anything for several hours and seems weak.
    • Crying nonstop that you can't settle, or acting "off" in a way that worries you.
    • Fever lasts more than 24 hours without a clear reason (no cold symptoms, no recent shots).

    If life-threatening, call 911 first, then Dad/Mom.

    Motrin (ibuprofen) dosing

    The bottle should have a chart by weight. If not, general guidance:

    • For a 5-year-old ~40 lbs: children's liquid (100 mg / 5 mL) — about 7.5 mL (1½ tsp), or chewable 100 mg tab.
    • Every 6 hours as needed. Do not give more than 4 doses in 24 hours.
    • Always give with a little food or milk if her stomach is touchy.
    • Avoid acetaminophen (Tylenol) for now unless Dad/Mom say so — sticking to one med makes the log cleaner.

    About this app

    Single-page tool. All entries live in this phone's browser storage — they don't go to the internet. If you clear browser history or use a different phone, the log resets.

    At end of day: tap export in the top right to text or email yourself today's log. That way Dad/Mom have a record even if anything is wiped.

    Quick reference

    • Normal: 97–100.4°F
    • Low-grade fever: 100.4–102°F — keep an eye on it
    • High fever: 102–104°F — give motrin, check in an hour
    • Call Dad/Mom: 104°F+ or anything from the warning list

    How the app works

    • 🌡 Log temperature — pick the number, pick a mood, add a note if you want.
    • 💊 Log motrin dose — pick the amount, pick a mood, hit save.
    • Each entry gets a time stamp automatically. You can edit the time if it's a "back-fill".
    • The status panel shows the last temp and last dose, and how long ago.
    • Reminders appear automatically — "next dose due around X" or "check temp in 45 min".
    • Tap the trash icon on any entry to delete it.
